MAKERELAB

Makerlab is a prototyping workshop for students of KEA. To use the workshop students have to
complete the intro-course held at Makerlab. Later in the semester Makerlab will also arrange machine specific courses where students gets hands on experience with the machines.

OPENING TIME

Workshop: Mon-Fri 09-16
Work area: Mon-Fri 09-22

GENERAL RULES

  • The workshop is ONLY or users associated with KEA.
  • User access require an intro course.
  • Access to machines are gained via booking system
  • Max. capacity for workshop is 35 users total.
  • Using the workshop machines are at your own risk.
  • User are urged to acquire health insurance.
  • You MUST comply with safety regulations.
  • NO food in the workshop.
  • Tools may never leave the workshop without special permission.
  • Storing material/projects in the lab is NOT allowed.
  • ALWAYS clean up - work must be stopped at least 15 min. before closing.

SAFETY RULES

  • Wear eye protection when cutting.
  • Wear ear protection.
  • When carrying heavy objects, wear safety shoes (acquired from the depo)
  • Long hair is tied up or wear a cap.
  • All loose clothing should be removed.

CLEANING

Machines has to be clean and ready for the next user before end of period, and latest 15 minutes before workshop closes.

BOOKING CALENDAR & REPOSITORY:

While waiting for our new home page and booking calendar we have set up a temporary booking system and in the repository you can also find tutorials and assets file needed to prepare your files.
http://kea-makerlab.github.io/hello

TIME LIMIT FOR BOOKING:

The machines listed above can only be booked from Monday to Friday, from 9am til 3.45pm. Students are allowed to book the machines up to 2 hours at a time and maximum 4 hours a week, with exception to the 3D printer, which often requires to run overnight. In this case you should book the printer both days.

CANCELLATION OF BOOKING

All bookings which should be modified or cancelled, are your own responsibility and can be made by logging into the calendar. If you need to cancel a booking, this should be done at least 24 hours in advance. If you haven’t cancelled your booking and don’t turn up, you will receive a warning. After 3 warnings you will be prohibited from using the workshop.

NB: The wood workshop doesn’t require a booking. It is free for use for those who have taken the intro-course, depending on capacity. The table saw and CNC machine cannot be used without the assistance of the workshop managers.
